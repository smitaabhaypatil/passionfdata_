3.1 Do you think that the population means for shingles A and B are equal? Form the hypothesis and conduct the test of the hypothesis. What assumption do you need to check before the test for equality of means is performed?

The independent samples t test and also called the unpaired samples t test. It helps you to compare the means of two sets of data.

In order to test whether there is a difference between population means, we are going to make assumptions: 1.The scale of measurement applied to data collected follows a continuous scale.The data are continuous. 2.Assumption of Independence:Both the samples are simple random samples,that the data collected from randomly selected portion of total population. The two Samples are independentof each other.Here Shingles A and Shingles B are independent variable. 3.Assumption of normality:The test-score would be dependent variable and it should be normally distributed. The data results in normal distribution ,bell -shaped distribution curve. 4.Sample size is between 16 and 40. 5.Assumption of Homogeneity of Variance:The variance of the two populations are equal. The independent t-test assumes the variances of the two independent variables you are measuring are equal in the population. Comparing Two Independent Population Means To compare independent samples, both populations are normally distributed with the population means and standard deviations unknown.

Step 1: Determine the Null and alternative hypotheses for the independent t-test:-

The null hypothesis for the independent t-test is that the population means from the two sampless are equal: H0: 1 = 2 Alternate hypothesis is that the population means are not equal: HA: 1!=2 These hypothesis constitute a two-tailed test. To do the hypothesis,we need a significance level also called alpha that allow us to either reject or accept the alternate hypothesis.Most probably the level of significance is 0.05.

Step 2: Decide the significance level/Formulate the analysis plan:

For this analysis, the significance level is 0.05. Using sample data, we will conduct a two-sample t-test of the null hypothesis. Sampling method was simple random sampling, the samples were independent, the sample size was much smaller than the population size,and the samples were drawn from a normal population.

Step 3: Identify the test statistic

We have two samples and we do not know the population standard deviation. Sample sizes for both samples are na=36 and nb=31 The sample size is between 16 and 40.So we can use the t distribution and t-test statistic for two samples unpaired test.

Step 4: Calculate the p-value and test statistic:

We use the scipy.stats.ttest_ind to calculate the t-test for the means of two independent samples of population. This function returns t statistic and two-tailed p value. This is a two-sided test for the null hypothesis that 2 independent samples have identical average (expected) values. This test assumes that the populations have identical variances.

3.2 What assumption about the population distribution is needed in order to conduct the hypothesis tests above?

Assumptions:

We assume that the population is approximately normally distributed and a random sample. A hypothesis test formally tests if the population the sample represents is normally-distributed.

The null hypothesis states that the population is normally distributed, against the alternative hypothesis that it is not normally-distributed. If the test p-value is less than the predefined significance level, you can reject the null hypothesis and conclude the data are not from a population with a normal distribution. If the p-value is greater than the predefined significance level, you cannot reject the null hypothesis.

Since in the above problem both samples Shingles A and Shingles B have a p-value (0.2017) above 0.05 (or 5 percent) level of significance,it can be concluded that both the samples are normally distributed.
